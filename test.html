GAMELOG

Hall of fame
Among the games that I've finished, these are the 10/10 ones that will stay forever in my mind as masterpieces.

NIER:AUTOMATA
⍟⍟⍟⍟⍟

Cyborgs fighting robots to save mankind. If you're familiar with Yoko Taro's works, you know how crazy and unexpected things get, and this is definetly his best work so far. The gameplay will change it up on you in flawless ways, the soundtrack is amazing, the story is sensational, and on top of it all, NIER:Automata is ALWAYS thinking outside the box. It's one of the games that will get your jaw on the floor thinking "woah, how did they thought of that?!" before you start crying.

Tip: There's a lot of endings, but only one of them is the final. Keep playing until you simply can't, and you won't miss it. Also, don't play it on a pirated copy (I don't care about legality/morality when it comes to piracy, but just trust me on this one, okay?)

HELLBLADE: SENUA'S SACRIFICE
⍟⍟⍟⍟⍟

Superb single player experience. Set in the late 8th century, you play as Senua, a girl on a journey to save the soul of her lover. Throughout the game you learn more and more about the protagonist, her "curse" that makes her see things and hear voices, the traumas she carries with her and many more. The game will occasionally break the 4th wall and use the things that happen to Senua as game mechanics in unique and creative ways, to fully imerse you in her place. They did such a good job on this, that sometimes you forget you're holding a controller and playing a videogame. A very special game, with a very special story about grief, mental health and self knowledge.

Tip: Use headphones for this one.

SUPER MARIO ODYSSEY
⍟⍟⍟⍟⍟

The best Mario game that has ever existed. Yes, you heard that right, it's THAT good.
It's like they gathered the best things that came out on different Mario games over the decades, and combined all of those here.
Perfectly flawless gameplay, with fluid animations on tight detailed movements, new mechanics that unveil things you didn't even thought it was possible to do, greatest post game I've ever played, charismatic characters on worlds that could've win awards on level design, all of this happening with one of the greatest videogame soundtracks of all time playing in the background.
It might be challenging as fuck, but it's never boring. Higly recommended.
